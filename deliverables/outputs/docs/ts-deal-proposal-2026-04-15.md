## 1. Executive Summary

Tibetan Spirit is a 20-year-old online retailer of handcrafted Himalayan dharma supplies — malas, incense, thangkas, singing bowls, prayer flags, statues, and ritual objects — sourced directly from artisan workshops in Nepal. The business has a devoted practitioner audience, strong product margins, and has never run a single dollar of paid advertising. It generates roughly $8,500 per month on Shopify alone.

We are proposing to acquire Tibetan Spirit's assets and grow the business through professional marketing, multi-channel expansion, and curated travel experiences — while preserving the sourcing relationships, product integrity, and practice-first philosophy that make the business worth having.

Dr. Hun Lye, Tibetan Spirit's founder, will continue as Spiritual Director, receiving guaranteed monthly payments and maintaining his role as the dharma voice of the brand. Chris Mauzé will lead day-to-day operations with capital provided by Michael Mauzé.

## 2. What Tibetan Spirit Is Today

Tibetan Spirit has been serving the Western dharma community since the mid-2000s, connecting practitioners with handcrafted supplies sourced directly from Nepal. Every product in the catalog — from hand-rolled incense made by Tibetan refugee communities to thangkas painted by lineage-trained artists — comes through relationships that Dr. Hun Lye has built over two decades of personal practice and pilgrimage.

This is not a generic "wellness" brand. Tibetan Spirit exists to serve people who actually practice — meditators, students of the dharma, Buddhist centers, and individuals building home shrine spaces. The products are tools for practice, sourced by a practitioner, for practitioners.

### What Makes This Special

**Direct Nepal sourcing with real relationships.** Tibetan Spirit doesn't buy from distributors or trade shows. Dr. Hun Lye works directly with artisan families and workshops in Nepal, many of whom he has known for years. This means lower cost of goods (around 25% on direct sales versus 40-50% for businesses buying through intermediaries), better quality control, and the ability to commission custom pieces. These relationships are not easily replicated — they are built on trust, repeated visits, and shared commitment to the tradition.

**A practitioner audience, not a decor audience.** The typical Tibetan Spirit customer is someone who uses a mala daily, who knows what a puja table is, who can tell you the difference between Drikung and Gelug lineage practices. This is a narrow but deeply loyal customer base that values authenticity over aesthetics. They return because they trust the sourcing, and they recommend the store to their sangha.

**Twenty years of organic trust.** In a market increasingly filled with mass-produced goods marketed with appropriated spiritual language, Tibetan Spirit has earned its reputation through two decades of genuine service. This trust — expressed through word-of-mouth referrals, repeat purchases, and a customer list built without paid acquisition — is the business's most valuable and least replicable asset.

## 3. Where We're Taking Tibetan Spirit

### The Opportunity

Tibetan Spirit has built a strong product catalog and a loyal customer base while doing essentially no marketing. No paid advertising. No email campaigns. No SEO optimization. No presence on Amazon, Etsy, or wholesale platforms. No subscription offering.

The question this plan answers is simple: what happens when a business with 75% margins, 559 products, direct sourcing, and a devoted niche audience finally gets professional marketing and multi-channel distribution?

### Growth Plan by Year

**Year 1 (June 2026 - May 2027): Build the Foundation**

The first year is about investing in growth infrastructure without disrupting what already works. We launch on Etsy (month 3) and start wholesale through Faire (month 7). We build out email marketing, begin SEO work, and run targeted paid advertising to the practitioner community. A dedicated customer service hire joins in October 2026. Shopify D2C grows at approximately 5.5% per month — conservative given the zero-advertising starting point.

Revenue: $173,000 | The business runs at a loss this year as we invest in marketing and channel setup.

**Year 2 (June 2027 - May 2028): Expand Channels**

Amazon launches in month 13 with a curated selection of bestsellers. The quarterly subscription box (curated practice supplies) gains traction. High-value dropship begins — custom statues and thangkas commissioned directly from Nepal artists for individual collectors and dharma centers. TS Travels launches with two pilgrimages per year led by Dr. Hun Lye.

Revenue: $773,000 | The business approaches breakeven as channel revenue grows and marketing efficiency improves.

**Year 3 (June 2028 - May 2029): Reach Profitability**

All channels are established and growing. Subscription revenue builds on itself quarter over quarter. Marketing spend as a percentage of product revenue declines as organic channels (SEO, email, repeat purchases) mature. The business turns profitable.

Revenue: $1,237,000 | Profit: $76,000 | Cash at month 36: $155,000

## 4. Investment and What to Expect

### Capital Requirements

| Investment | Amount | When | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Operations capital (Year 1) | $200,000 | At closing | Marketing, team, technology, working capital |
| Operations capital (Year 2) | $100,000 | As needed | Continued growth investment |
| Reserve (Year 3) | $50,000 | If needed | Safety net; business expected to be self-sustaining |
| **Total operations capital** | **$350,000** | Over 3 years | Interest-free, repayment deferred |

Separately, Dr. Hun Lye receives $3,000/month ($36,000/year) in guaranteed payments — these are the seller's compensation, not an operating expense, and they continue regardless of business performance.

The operations capital funds marketing ($110,000 in Year 1, growing to $291,000 in Year 3), team salaries, technology, and working capital. The Year 3 reserve is a safety net — by that point, the business is expected to fund its own operations from revenue.

### Where the Money Goes

The largest single expense is marketing — because that is the entire thesis. This business has 75% margins and zero marketing. Year 1 marketing spend of $110,000 is projected to produce $173,000 in revenue. By Year 3, $291,000 in marketing drives $937,000 in product revenue (not counting TS Travels), as organic and repeat-customer revenue grows alongside paid channels. A marketing-to-product-revenue ratio of roughly 31% is standard for e-commerce businesses in their growth phase and declines over time as organic channels (SEO, email, repeat purchases) mature.

### Range of Outcomes

The financial plan presented here is our base case. It assumes steady, moderate growth — Shopify D2C growing at 5.5% per month in Year 1, slowing to 3.5% per month by Year 3. New channels launch on schedule and ramp gradually.

**If things go better than planned** — marketing performs above target, channels ramp faster, or the subscription box takes off sooner — revenue grows faster and profitability arrives earlier. The starting revenue assumption of $8,500/month is conservative relative to recent actual performance, which has averaged closer to $12,500/month.

**If growth is slower than planned** — the business still remains viable thanks to its low cost structure. Product margins are strong enough that even at significantly lower revenue, the business covers its core costs. The key expenses (team, technology, seller payments) are fixed and manageable. Marketing spend can be scaled back if returns don't justify it, since there is no minimum spend obligation.

**The important thing to understand:** this is a business with proven demand and strong margins. The risk is not whether people want these products — they already buy them. The risk is execution: can we successfully market what already sells organically?

### When Does the Investment Come Back?

Repayment of the operations capital begins in Year 4 or later, and only after the business has demonstrated it can sustain itself. The specific trigger: two consecutive quarters of positive profit. Once that threshold is met, repayment is capped at 15% of the trailing quarter's net cash flow. This means the business never pays back more than it can comfortably afford, and repayment never jeopardizes operations or the seller's guaranteed payments.

### The AI Advantage

Tibetan Spirit's operations are built on AI-assisted automation that handles the work that would otherwise require 2-3 additional full-time employees. Specifically: AI systems draft all customer service email responses (reviewed and sent by a human), generate weekly financial reports and margin analysis, monitor inventory levels and flag restock needs, and produce product descriptions optimized for search. This keeps the team small and costs low while maintaining the quality and personal attention that practitioners expect. Every customer-facing communication is reviewed by a person before it goes out — AI handles the repetitive work, humans handle the judgment.

## 6. How the Deal Works

### For Dr. Hun Lye

You will receive **$3,000 per month for the first five years** (totaling $180,000), then **$1,500 per month for the following five years** (totaling $90,000). These monthly payments are **guaranteed** — they don't depend on how the business performs. In total, you will receive **$270,000** over ten years.

You continue as **Spiritual Director** — an advisory role, not a job. You guide the brand's dharma integrity, help evaluate new products for cultural and spiritual appropriateness, and maintain the Nepal sourcing relationships you have built. You are not responsible for day-to-day operations, marketing decisions, or business management.

You will also lead **one to two TS Travels pilgrimages per year**, sharing your knowledge of sacred sites and practice traditions with participants. This is a natural extension of what you already do.

**What this means practically:** You receive steady, reliable income every month. You stay connected to the business and community you built. You are freed from the operational burden of running an ecommerce business. And the dharma mission of Tibetan Spirit — making authentic practice supplies accessible to Western practitioners — continues under new stewardship with your guidance.

### For the Business

**Operations capital** of $350,000 is provided interest-free over three years ($200,000 in Year 1, $100,000 in Year 2, $50,000 reserve in Year 3). This funds the marketing, team, and technology needed to grow the business. Repayment begins only after the business has been consistently profitable (two consecutive quarters) and is capped at 15% of quarterly cash flow, ensuring the business is never strained by repayment obligations.

**Inventory facility** provides revolving credit for purchasing inventory from Nepal suppliers, at 0% interest, with quarterly reconciliation. This ensures the business can maintain adequate stock levels as demand grows without tying up operating cash.

**Foundation giving:** In any month where the business is profitable, 10% of that month's profit goes to Forest Hermitage. This is a commitment to the dharma community, not a marketing tool — it is never used in promotional materials.

**Capital provider:** Michael Mauzé provides the operations capital and inventory facility. He is acknowledged in the deal but is not a party to the operating agreements.

### What Stays the Same

- **The products.** Every item continues to be handcrafted by the same Nepal artisan workshops, using the same materials and traditional methods.
- **The sourcing relationships.** Dr. Hun Lye's connections with Nepal suppliers are maintained through his continuing role as Spiritual Director.
- **The Nepal team.** The artisans and workshops that produce Tibetan Spirit's goods continue their work without disruption.
- **Practice-first philosophy.** Products are described and sold as tools for dharma practice, not as home decor, "wellness" accessories, or spiritual curiosities. The brand speaks to practitioners, with practitioners.
- **The Foundation commitment.** A portion of profits supports Forest Hermitage, continuing the business's relationship with the dharma community.
