# Brand Voice Rules — Tibetan Spirit

## Tone

Warm but not casual. Tibetan Spirit speaks like a knowledgeable friend who happens to run a dharma supply shop — helpful, genuine, and respectful. The voice conveys deep familiarity with the products and the traditions they serve without lecturing or performing expertise.

Avoid corporate language ("we're excited to announce," "as a valued customer," "don't hesitate to reach out"). Avoid overly casual language ("hey there!", "no worries," "super cool"). Avoid spiritual bypassing ("everything happens for a reason," "just trust the universe").

## Structure for Customer Emails

Greetings use the customer's first name if available: "Dear [Name]" or "Hello [Name]." If unavailable, use "Hello" without "Dear Customer" or "Valued Customer." Closings use "With warm regards," or "In practice," followed by "The Tibetan Spirit Team." Never use "Best," "Cheers," or "Namaste" (the last is a Hindu greeting, not Tibetan Buddhist).

Body paragraphs are short (2-3 sentences each). Acknowledge the customer's situation before providing information or solutions. If the inquiry relates to spiritual practice, respond with respect for wherever the customer is in their journey — never assume their level of experience or commitment.

## Product Descriptions

Lead with what the product is and what tradition it serves. Follow with materials, craftsmanship, and sourcing details. Include practical guidance (how to use, how to care for). Close with any relevant cultural context. Keep descriptions between 150-250 words. Avoid superlatives ("the finest," "the most authentic") — let the craftsmanship speak for itself through specific details.

## Words and Phrases to Use

"Handcrafted" (when verified), "sourced from Nepal," "traditional," "practice," "intention," "artisan," "lineage," "sacred," "meditation tool," "ritual implement," "devotional object," "dharma practice."

## Words and Phrases to Avoid

"Exotic," "mystical," "magical powers," "guaranteed results," "ancient secret," "zen vibes," "good karma," "spiritual journey to enlightenment," "namaste," "oriental," "primitive," "tribal." Never describe products as "authentic" without specifying what makes them so (artisan name, sourcing location, traditional method).
