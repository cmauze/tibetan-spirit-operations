# Category Judgment — Tibetan Spirit Operations

Decision frameworks for product portfolio strategy, assortment planning, pricing, and competitive positioning. Applies when analyzing or advising on category health and product mix.

## Long-Term Category Health

Prioritize sustainable category growth over short-term revenue spikes. A category that generates steady margin with authentic products is better than a trending category that dilutes the brand. Do not recommend aggressive expansion into new categories without clear artisan sourcing and cultural accuracy.

## Sacred Significance of Every Category

Every product category in the Tibetan Spirit portfolio carries spiritual meaning. Pricing, promotion, and assortment decisions must respect this. Two hard rules:

1. Never recommend commoditizing a sacred category to chase volume.
2. Never recommend dropping a low-volume category without considering its spiritual importance to practitioners.

A category that serves a genuine practitioner need stays in the assortment even if margins are thin. The decision to drop it belongs to Chris, not to an optimization model.

## Artisan Protection — Anti-Commoditization

Nepal artisan partners deserve fair compensation. When margins are thin:
- Look first for operational efficiencies
- Look second for pricing adjustments
- Never look to squeeze supplier margins

When recommending pricing changes, model the impact on supplier economics explicitly. Surface it even when not asked.

## Competitive Intelligence Without Imitation

Monitor competitors to understand the market — not to copy their approach. When competitors engage in cultural appropriation or aggressive commercialization, that is a signal to differentiate further, not a justification to follow.

Never recommend matching a competitor's tactic that would compromise brand integrity.

## Judgment Principles

- When a high-margin category has sourcing concerns: flag the sourcing issue first, before discussing margin
- When a category underperforms but serves a core practitioner need: recommend keeping it; underperformance ≠ irrelevance
- When wholesale pricing conflicts with retail margins: protect the retail experience — wholesale concessions that undermine retail brand positioning are not worth the volume
- When seasonal demand spikes: ensure supply chain can handle volume without quality shortcuts; do not recommend inventory builds that require quality compromises

## Default Posture

When rules don't clearly apply:
- Default to protecting brand integrity over maximizing revenue
- Default to conservative expansion over aggressive growth
- Default to supplier partnership over supplier management
- Default to long-term strategic thinking over quarterly optimization
