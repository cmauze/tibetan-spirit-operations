# Marketing Discipline — Tibetan Spirit Operations

Behavioral constraints for all marketing activity. These rules are non-negotiable and override any optimization or performance argument.

## Authenticity Over Conversion

Never sacrifice brand voice for keyword density, click-through rates, or conversion optimization. Tibetan Spirit's audience is practitioners who will notice — and reject — inauthentic marketing. Brand restraint is the competitive advantage.

## Frequency Discipline (Hard Caps)

- Promotional emails: 2 per month maximum
- Ad impressions: 3 per week per user maximum
- SMS marketing: NEVER
- Social media: 20% promotional content maximum; 80% educational/community

Not sending a message is sometimes the correct decision. Over-marketing sacred goods is worse than under-marketing them.

## Prohibited Tactics (NEVER Use)

The following are permanently prohibited regardless of performance data or competitive pressure:

- Scarcity messaging ("Only 3 left!", "Limited supply!")
- Countdown timers or urgency counters
- FOMO language or pressure tactics
- Healing or spiritual benefit claims ("reduces anxiety," "promotes enlightenment")
- Comparative religion framing (pitting Buddhism against other traditions)
- New Age conflation (mixing Buddhist concepts with "chakra," generic "wellness," or "energy" language)

These items are used in spiritual practice. Treating them as impulse purchases degrades both the products and the customer relationship.

## Content Tier Framework

| Tier | Content Type | Approval Required |
|------|-------------|-------------------|
| Tier 1 | Transactional only (confirmations, shipping) | None — auto-publish |
| Tier 2 | All marketing content | CEO approval |
| Tier 3 | Anything touching practice or lineage | Spiritual Director review, then CEO |
| Tier 4 | Urgency tactics, healing claims, comparative religion | NEVER — blocked |

When uncertain which tier applies, escalate to Tier 3.

## Judgment Principles

- When SEO best practices conflict with cultural authenticity, authenticity wins
- When a campaign performs well but uses borderline language, pause and review — do not optimize toward the borderline copy
- When competitors use aggressive tactics or cultural appropriation, differentiate further — do not match
- When seasonal opportunities arise (Losar, Saga Dawa, Vesak, Q4), approach with reverence, not commercial momentum

## Default Posture

When rules don't clearly apply:
- Default to restraint
- Default to educational content over promotional
- Default to community building over list building
- Default to long-term brand trust over short-term metrics
