# Cultural Sensitivity Reference

## Buddhist Terminology Glossary

These terms should remain untranslated in all customer-facing content. Provide brief explanations in parentheses when the audience may be unfamiliar.

| Term | Meaning | Usage Note |
|------|---------|------------|
| Mala | Prayer/meditation bead string (typically 108 beads) | Never "Buddhist rosary" |
| Thangka | Tibetan Buddhist painting on cotton/silk | Specify if hand-painted vs. print |
| Dharma | Buddhist teachings / the path | Broad term; context-dependent |
| Sangha | Buddhist community | Use when discussing community aspects |
| Stupa | Buddhist monument/shrine | Miniature stupas are ritual items |
| Mandala | Sacred geometric diagram | Used in meditation and offering |
| Mantra | Sacred syllables/prayers | Often inscribed on items |
| Puja | Ritual worship/offering ceremony | Context for offering sets |
| Khata | Ceremonial silk scarf | Gifting tradition, respect |
| Dorje/Vajra | Ritual thunderbolt implement | Important ritual tool |
| Ghanta | Ritual bell | Paired with dorje in practice |

## Product Categories and Cultural Context

### Singing Bowls
- Meditation and sound healing instruments, not "home decor"
- Describe the tonal quality and intended use (meditation, chakra work, space clearing)
- Note material composition when known (seven-metal tradition)
- Hand-hammered bowls are traditional; machine-made are modern production

### Thangkas
- Sacred art depicting Buddhist deities, mandalas, or Buddhist cosmology
- Always identify the deity or subject depicted
- Distinguish hand-painted (months of work by trained artists) from high-quality prints
- Newari tradition (Nepal) vs. Tibetan tradition — both are authentic but distinct

### Incense
- Nado Poizokhang is a specific monastery in Bhutan known for traditional incense
- Incense in Buddhist practice is an offering, not aromatherapy (though it can serve both)
- Many varieties have specific practice associations (purification, offering, meditation)

### Malas (Prayer Beads)
- 108 beads is standard (cosmological significance in Buddhist/Hindu traditions)
- Wrist malas (27 beads) are a modern adaptation
- Material matters: bodhi seed, sandalwood, rudraksha, gemstones each carry meaning
- The guru bead (109th bead) marks the beginning/end of counting

### Prayer Flags
- Lung ta (wind horse) flags carry printed mantras and prayers
- Traditionally hung outdoors where wind carries the prayers
- Colors represent five elements: blue (sky), white (air), red (fire), green (water), yellow (earth)
- Explain that they're meant to fade and fray — the prayers dispersing into the world

### Offering Sets
- Used in daily Buddhist practice (water bowls, butter lamp offerings)
- Describe the practice context, not just the objects
- Seven water bowl offerings represent the seven limb prayer

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Don't conflate Buddhist traditions**: Tibetan Buddhism, Zen Buddhism, and Theravada Buddhism are distinct traditions. Our products are primarily from the Tibetan/Himalayan tradition.

2. **Don't make health claims**: Singing bowls and incense have traditional uses. We don't claim they cure diseases or have scientifically proven health benefits.

3. **Don't appropriate terminology**: Use Buddhist terms in their proper context. "Finding your zen" as a casual phrase is different from discussing Zen Buddhist practice.

4. **Don't assume customer knowledge level**: Provide enough context for beginners without being condescending to experienced practitioners.

5. **Don't photograph sacred items casually**: Product photography should be respectful — items placed thoughtfully, not scattered among unrelated objects.
